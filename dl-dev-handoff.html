Development Handoff

Bridging design and development through collaboration, documentation, and mutual understanding

My Role
Lead Designer
Context
IT background, cross-functional
Focus
Design-to-dev collaboration
01 — Overview

A natural bridge between design and code

Coming from an IT background, I was comfortable communicating with developers and understanding technical constraints. This helped create smoother collaboration between design and development during the handoff phase.

The goal was never just to deliver files — it was to ensure developers had everything they needed to build with confidence, without having to interpret or guess.

02 — Collaborative Handoff Process

Building alignment before execution

Once designs were finalised, we conducted joint walkthrough sessions with developers:

  • We reviewed each feature together
  • Discussed flows, edge cases, and possible technical limitations
  • Restructured certain interactions where needed
  • Collected and addressed implementation feedback early
The outcome
These discussions helped reduce back-and-forth during development and ensured better alignment before execution began.
03 — Handling Disagreements

Knowing when to hold and when to adapt

Naturally, there were moments of disagreement.

At times, certain design decisions could not be compromised due to usability or business impact. In other situations, adjustments were necessary to meet technical feasibility or timelines.

Balancing ideal design solutions with practical constraints was sometimes challenging. It required flexibility, clear reasoning, and knowing when to adapt — and when to stand firm.

04 — Clear Documentation & Organised Files

Files built for implementation

To support a smooth transition:

  • I ensured Figma files were clean, structured, and easy to navigate
  • Components and screens were properly organised
  • Notes were added wherever clarification was needed
  • Additional documentation was provided when required
05 — Reflection

What this process reinforced

This process strengthened my ability to bridge design and development. I learned that successful handoff is not just about delivering files — it's about conversation, clarity, and mutual understanding.

What I took away: Smooth collaboration leads to fewer surprises, better builds, and happier teams on both sides.
