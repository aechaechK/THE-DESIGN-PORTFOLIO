Client Customisations

Tailoring solutions for enterprise clients without compromising product integrity

My Role
Lead Designer
Context
Enterprise client requests
Scope
Design, feedback, handoff
01 — Overview

Designing beyond the core product

Alongside core product design, I also worked on client-specific customisations. When clients requested changes or additional features, I designed screens accordingly to help them clearly visualise the proposed solution before development began.

The challenge was always the same: deliver what the client needs, without compromising the integrity of the core product or the design system underpinning it.

02 — Design & Feedback Process

From requirement to approval

  • Created high-fidelity mockups based on client requirements
  • Walked clients through the design decisions and user flow
  • Gathered detailed feedback
  • Refined designs based on discussions
  • Finalised the approved version and handed it off to developers
Why this mattered
This process ensured alignment before implementation and reduced major revisions later.
03 — The Initial Challenge

Communicating design decisions with confidence

The most challenging part in the beginning was communicating my design decisions confidently and clearly.

It wasn't just about showing a screen — it was about explaining:

  • Why certain layouts were structured a specific way
  • How the design supported their business goals
  • Why some elements were prioritised over others

Clients naturally had their own expectations and perspectives. At times, their vision differed from the proposed design direction. In some cases, we refined the solution together; in others, we aligned with their preference while ensuring the core objective remained intact.

04 — Balancing Vision & Practicality

Learning to guide and adapt

Over time, I improved at:

  • Presenting design decisions with clear reasoning
  • Connecting design choices directly to business outcomes
  • Listening carefully to client concerns before responding
  • Knowing when to advocate strongly — and when to align with the client's vision

Learning when to guide and when to adapt became an important skill for me.

05 — Reflection

Design is only part of the job

Client customisations taught me that good design is not only about aesthetics or usability — it's also about communication, trust, and alignment.

What I took away: Designing is one part of the job. Helping clients understand and believe in the design is equally important.
